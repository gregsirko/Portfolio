United Cargo

Cargo shipment mobile app for United Airlines

Company

United Airlines

Role

UX Designer

Duration

4 months

Team

Purdue UX

The Product

A mobile app for cargo shipping.

The air-freight division of United Airlines, United Cargo operates via seven major U.S. hubs and a global network that reaches over 100 destinations.

The problem

United Cargo operated only two different web tools for their cargo management side of business, whose unresponsive nature on mobile devices hindered and eliminated further possible use cases and experiences.

The outcome

With a team, I delivered a mobile app experience that bridged a critical mobile computing opportunity.

Context

Students partner with industry sponsors.

In my undergraduate program at Purdue UX Design, cohorts gained exposure to industry settings through different real-world problem prompts every semester.

Persona

Cargo managers need mobile computing power.

The operational habits of target users, cargo managers, needs to account for a lot of in the field and on the feet app usage. A smart, easy, and deceptively powerful app needs to exist.

Updated Product

United Cargo is currently held back by an old website.

The current United Cargo eCommerce web platform that cargo managers use is very outdated with a clunky UI and brand and lack of responsive layouts.

RESEARCH APPROACH

Industry-established foundations guided strong mobile design in key interface areas.

RESEARCH

Best eCommerce Practices

Mobile must be intuitive

A competitive analysis of leading industry analogues for shipment processing and tracking led the team to a few key insights. Large, obvious UIs exist with clear, easily pressable buttons and smooth flows.

Keep forms quick and concise

Robust secondary research led me to understand the importance of form design. The journey needs to be broken up across different screens where possible, as users primarily like to tap and advance on mobile contexts.

Broken web platform

The existing web tools are an outdated, unsuable platform that don't fit into a modern lifestyle with mobile computing in unexpected contexts. Our team gleaned the primary core functionalities of the website to uphold in our own design.

DESIGN APPROACH

The outdated web platform content needed to be restructured and prepared for a new look.

ITERATIVE DESIGN

Locking in the UI

Sketching user flows

Our team took the core functionalities of cargo booking, shipping and tracking, as well as viewing flight schedules and began to sketch out striaghtforward user flows around these.

Wireframes for key actions

The best features from sketches were grouped and chosen through affinity diagramming to align our key core features. Low-fidelity wireframes then gave shape to these actions to start forming the app skeleton.

Usbility testing to optimize experience

With higher levels of UI fidelity being added and a brand design system taking shape, our team had proper prototypes to test and present in front of users. Users proposed changes to different small and easy-to-miss details around flights and adding pieces.

DELIVERABLE

After reviewing final insights from interface usability testing, I refined high-fidelity prototypes around core shipment-based actions.

DELIVERABLE

Easy Mobile Experience

01

View available flight schedules

Users can view a feed of availabe flights on which their shipments can be packed and transported. Clear headers and comfortable card style help to create an intuitive mobile selection process.

02

Add items to shipment

Forms allows users to punch in key details around the items within their cargo shipment. A series of smaller, chunked steps help to lighten up the cognitive load in making decisions.

03

Review booking details

After adding different bookings for cargo shipment, users can review their details. Comfortable buttons and dropdowns allows a pleasant experience for the thumbs. Users now have checkpoints to either edit of they made a mistake or delete different pieces.

04

Track a shipment across space and time

After booking a cargo shipment, users can track and analyze key details of the process. A streamlined UI shows only the most pertinent details of the flight tracking journey.
